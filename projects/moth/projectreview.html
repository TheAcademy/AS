The Moth Project

The moth project was the fourth and largest project the freshman class did. The project was split into two parts, a historical half and a half focused around  the class’s personal stories. The historical part of the project was based around the Civil Rights Movement, the class had gotten an introduction to that  during the movie review project. The students were separated into groups and were told to research a topic based around the Civil Rights `Movement (topics such as the Ruby Bridges story, or the freedom riders) after the students gathered what they felt like was enough info, they made presentations around their topic. Those presentations were printed and compiled into a timeline outside the room. One video the class watched in preparation of their personal stories was about an african american woman named Stephanie Summerville, who told them a story of how she had a run-in with a member of the KKK (Ku Klux Klan) in Indiana. She had gotten a nursing job where she would take care of very sick people in their homes. Stephanie’s first job was to help take care of an old man who was part of the KKK clan, alongside his wife.

During the personal story part of the project, students had write and recite a true and memorable moment from their lives. Their stories had to be memorized, and were going to be recorded and played on the radio. By the end of the project, everyone had a story that they shared in front of the class. Overall, this project was good way for students to look back on something, good or bad, in our own lives and their classmates lives.
